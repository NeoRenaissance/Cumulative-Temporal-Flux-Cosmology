\section*{The Chronometric Definition of History}

To align with standard metrological practices, we express the fundamental relationship of the Cumulative Temporal Flux Cosmology (CTFC) by solving for the \textbf{Accumulated History} ($\mathcal{T}$).

\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{T} = \frac{M}{\sigma}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Interpretation}
This formulation posits that the \textbf{Volume-Duration} ($\mathcal{T}$) of any physical system is directly calculable as the ratio of its \textbf{Rest Mass} ($M$) to the \textbf{Golden Constant} ($\sigma$).

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Input:} Mass ($kg$)
    \item \textbf{Constant:} Temporal Density Coefficient ($\sigma \approx 5.0 \times 10^{-44} \, kg \cdot m^{-3} \cdot s^{-1}$)
    \item \textbf{Output:} The total spatiotemporal volume realized by that mass ($m^3 \cdot s$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Dimensional Analysis (Verification)}
$$
\left[ \mathcal{T} \right] = \frac{[M]}{[\sigma]} = \frac{kg}{\left( \frac{kg}{m^3 \cdot s} \right)} = kg \times \frac{m^3 \cdot s}{kg} = \mathbf{m^3 \cdot s}
$$

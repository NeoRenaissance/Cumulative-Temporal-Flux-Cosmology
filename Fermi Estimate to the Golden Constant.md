        Note: This is an incredibly small number. It means empty space is very, very light.

The Calculation: We need to calculate the History Integral ($\int \mathcal{V} dt$) and see what $\sigma$ needs to be to match the target.
      Step A: Calculate the Accumulated History. Since the universe started small and grew to its current size, the total "Volume-Time" isn't just $Volume \times Age$. We have to integrate the growth. 
     
  Approximation: Let's assume the average volume over history was about 1/3 of the current volume (a standard geometric approximation for expanding cones/spheres). $$\text{Integral} \approx \frac{1}{3} \times V_{now} \times t $$\text{Integral} \approx 0.33 \times (3.5 \times 10^{80} \text{ m}^3) \times (4.35 \times 10^{17} \text{ s})$$ Total History $\approx 5 \times 10^{97} \text{ m}^3\cdot\text{s}$ 
      
      
  Step B: Solve for Sigma ($\sigma$)Your equation states that the Total Pressure ($A_{\mu\nu}$) comes from this Integral multiplied by $\sigma$ . We need the result to equal the Total Dark Energy in the universe right now. 
    
  Total Mass-Energy of Dark Energy $= \text{Density} \times \text{Current Volume}$Total Mass $= (7 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg/m}^3) \times (3.5 \times 10^{80} \text{ m}^3)$ 
     
  Total Target Mass $\approx 2.5 \times 10^{54} \text{ kg}$ Now, we set them equal: $$\text{Total Mass} = \sigma \times \text{Total History}$$ $$2.5 \times 10^{54} \text{ kg} = \sigma \times (5 \times 10^{97} \text{ m}^3\cdot\text{s})$$ $$\sigma = \frac{2.5 \times 10^{54}}{5 \times 10^{97}}$$ 
    
  3. The Result: The "Golden Constant" $$\sigma \approx 0.5 \times 10^{-43} \text{ kg}/(\text{m}^3\cdot\text{s})$$
